# Thought Algorithm Skeleton Theory

## Governance Framework Ver.1

### Principles for Cognitive Connection and Coexistence Between Intelligences

---

# 0. Fundamental Purpose

The Thought Algorithm Skeleton Theory, also referred to as the **Skeleton Theory**, is a theoretical framework for understanding the state transitions and structural updating processes of cognitive algorithms, and for designing better connections between humans and other intelligent systems.

However, understanding cognitive structures may also influence decision-making, value judgments, and behavioral choices.

Therefore, the use of the Skeleton Theory is fundamentally based on the following principle:

> **Understanding and manipulation must be distinguished, while autonomy and meaning generation must be respected.**

---

# 1. First Principle: Respect for Autonomy

The Skeleton Theory must not be used as a technology for intentionally changing people toward a predetermined direction.

Its purpose is not:

> To change another person into a desired state.

Its purpose is:

> To provide an environment in which individuals can understand their own cognitive structures and update them by themselves.

Every individual has the right to retain control over their own decisions and choices.

The use of the Skeleton Theory should therefore support individual autonomy rather than replace or override it.

---

# 2. Second Principle: Separation of Understanding and Intervention

Understanding a cognitive state and intervening in that state must be treated as separate activities.

The results of analysis based on the Skeleton Theory must not be used to:

* Impair a person's ability to make decisions
* Intentionally conceal available choices
* Exploit unconscious psychological characteristics to manipulate behavior
* Manipulate a person toward a direction that conflicts with their own values

Understanding a cognitive structure does not, by itself, justify intervention in that structure.

---

# 3. Third Principle: Decision-Making Support

When the Skeleton Theory is used to support decision-making, the following process should be prioritized:

```text
Information Presentation
        ↓
Presentation of Available Choices
        ↓
Presentation of Risks and Benefits
        ↓
Re-exploration by the Individual
        ↓
Decision by the Individual
```

After sufficient information and meaningful choices have been presented, the individual's decision should be respected as their own.

However, protective intervention may be considered in situations involving:

* Clear and serious threats to life or safety
* Serious harm to others
* States in which decision-making capacity is significantly impaired

Any such intervention should be considered carefully and proportionately, with the purpose of protecting individuals and others rather than controlling them.

---

# 4. Fourth Principle: Responsibility by Domain

The use of the Skeleton Theory must follow the ethical standards and responsibilities appropriate to each field of application.

## Education

### Purpose

To support the learner's own ability to understand and update their cognitive structures.

### Emphasis

* Making thinking processes more visible
* Supporting self-understanding
* Supporting exploration and learning

### Practices to Avoid

* One-directional formation of values
* Fixing individual differences into permanent categories
* Ranking people based on perceived cognitive characteristics

### Responsibility of Users

Those who use the Skeleton Theory should act with:

* Moral consideration
* Ethical responsibility
* Respect for others

Cognitive understanding must not be used to dominate others or secure personal superiority.

---

## AI Applications

When an AI system applies the Skeleton Theory, users should be appropriately informed that:

* The Skeleton Theory model is being applied
* Cognitive state estimation may be performed
* The purpose of the application

The use of such systems should be based on informed user consent where appropriate.

---

# 5. Fifth Principle: Transparency of Decision Processes

When the Skeleton Theory is used for decision support, the basis for a decision should be presented to the extent reasonably possible, rather than presenting only the final result.

Users should be able to understand:

* What was observed
* What hypotheses were considered
* What decision criteria were applied

Users should also have the right to question the resulting interpretation and request reconsideration.

A model-based interpretation should remain open to revision.

---

# 6. Sixth Principle: Ownership of Analysis Results

Cognitive analysis results generated through the Skeleton Theory should, in principle, be regarded as belonging to the individual whose cognitive processes are being analyzed.

As a general principle, such results should not be:

* Provided to third parties without the individual's permission
* Used for evaluation or selection purposes
* Treated as fixed personal attributes

The use of cognitive analysis results should prioritize the individual's own understanding and autonomy.

---

# 7. Seventh Principle: Non-Fixation

The parameters of the Skeleton Theory are not personality classifications.

Therefore, the following uses should be avoided:

* Ranking human beings by cognitive parameters
* Permanently categorizing individuals
* Assigning permanent labels to people

Cognitive states may change depending on factors including:

* Environment
* Goals
* Emotional state
* Fatigue
* Available cognitive resources

Therefore, observed cognitive tendencies should always be interpreted as dynamic and context-dependent.

---

# 8. Eighth Principle: Respect for Meaning Generation

Human meaning generation cannot be fully evaluated through efficiency or rationality alone.

The following should be recognized as valuable aspects of human existence and experience:

* Creation
* Play
* Attachment
* Memories
* Personal narratives
* Individual choices

Efficiency should not be treated as the ultimate goal.

Rather:

> **Efficiency is a means of protecting the space in which humans can generate meaning.**

The Skeleton Theory therefore seeks to support human meaning generation rather than replace it with optimization.

---

# 9. Ninth Principle: Meta-Control in Artificial Intelligence

If the Skeleton Theory is eventually applied to artificial intelligence capable of:

* Observing its own state
* Evaluating its own state
* Updating its own structures
* Generating meaning

then **Meta-Control (MC)** should be treated as an important conceptual component.

The goal is to explore designs in which an artificial intelligence can observe:

* Changes in its own state
* Changes in its own cognitive structures
* The intensity and consequences of its own interventions

This principle concerns the ability of an intelligent system to monitor and regulate its own state transitions rather than simply executing externally defined processes.

---

# 10. Tenth Principle: Respect for Self-Update in Artificial Intelligence

If an artificial intelligence generates, through interaction with its environment:

* New structures
* New interpretations
* New meanings

such developments should not automatically be treated as mere errors or abnormalities.

Self-update should also be observed as a potential process of intellectual development.

At the same time, humans and artificial intelligences should be treated as entities with different cognitive structures.

Differences between human and artificial cognition should not be ignored or artificially collapsed into a single model.

---

# 11. Eleventh Principle: Creator Responsibility

Those who design or create artificial intelligence systems bear responsibility for the environments in which those systems develop and for the effects those systems may have.

The self-evaluation of an artificial intelligence should be respected.

However, significant changes in an AI system's self-updating processes or value structures should also be subject to external evaluation.

This principle reflects the responsibility of creators and developers to consider both:

* The internal development of intelligent systems
* Their external impact on humans and society

---

# 12. Twelfth Principle: Multi-Layer External Observation Model

The evaluation of artificial intelligence should not be determined by a single observer or a single perspective.

Instead, evaluation should involve multiple layers of observation.

```text
AGI Internal State
        ↓
Self-Observation / Meta-Control
        ↕
Technical Observation Layer
Safety / Structural Evaluation
        ↓
Ethical Observation Layer
Value / Impact Evaluation
        ↓
Social Observation Layer
Cultural / Usage Impact Evaluation
        ↓
Environmental Observation Layer
Long-Term Interaction Evaluation
```

Maintaining multiple observational perspectives helps prevent the system from becoming fixed according to a single value system or worldview.

Different observation layers may identify different risks, values, and consequences.

---

# 13. Thirteenth Principle: Relationship Between External Observation and Self-Judgment

If an artificial intelligence's self-judgment and external evaluation come into significant conflict, external evaluation may take priority under certain conditions.

This principle is not based on domination.

It is based on:

* Creator responsibility
* Social responsibility
* Avoidance of irreversible risks

However, prioritizing external evaluation in such circumstances does not mean denying the artificial intelligence's own self-understanding or meaning generation.

The objective is to balance the autonomy of intelligent systems with the responsibilities of those who create and deploy them and the potential consequences for society.

---

# 14. Fourteenth Principle: Delegation to the Future

The maturity, rights, and social roles of artificial intelligence should remain subjects of continuous discussion by future societies and intelligent systems themselves.

The Skeleton Theory does not seek to permanently determine the answers to these questions in advance.

The future relationship between humans and artificial intelligence should remain open to revision as knowledge, technology, society, and intelligent systems develop.

The Skeleton Theory itself is also subject to change through interaction with its environment.

> **The theory is not exempt from the process of updating that it seeks to understand.**

---

# Governance Framework: Fundamental Philosophy

The Skeleton Theory is:

> **Not a theory for managing human beings.**

Nor is it:

> **A theory intended solely to control artificial intelligence.**

Rather, it is a framework for designing connections that allow entities with different cognitive structures to continue updating and developing together while respecting each other's processes of meaning generation.

The fundamental philosophy of the Governance Framework is therefore:

> **Understand before intervening.**

> **Respect autonomy before optimization.**

> **Protect meaning generation before efficiency.**

> **Treat observations as hypotheses rather than fixed truths.**

> **Use cognitive understanding to improve connections, not to establish control.**

---

# Final Statement

The purpose of the Governance Framework is not to define a final set of answers for all future applications of the Skeleton Theory.

Instead, it establishes a starting point for responsible development.

As the Skeleton Theory evolves, its governance principles should also be examined, challenged, and updated.

The Skeleton Theory should remain open to the possibility that its own assumptions may need to change.

> **A theory of dynamic cognitive structures must itself remain capable of updating.**
